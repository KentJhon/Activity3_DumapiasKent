About Japanese Restau


Tradition since 1889

In the bustling streets of Tokyo's historic Asakusa district, nestled amidst the traditional architecture and vibrant culture, lies Japanese Restau, a culinary haven with a rich history spanning nearly four decades. Founded in 1985 by husband-and-wife duo Hiroshi and Emiko Tanaka, Japanese Restau was born out of their shared passion for preserving and sharing the authentic flavors of Japanese cuisine. Both hailing from families deeply rooted in culinary traditions, Hiroshi, with his expertise in traditional cooking techniques, and Emiko, with her keen eye for hospitality, embarked on a journey to create a dining experience that would captivate the senses and nurture the soul. We only use fresh ingredients.

Our Menu


Sushi

Sushi is perhaps Japan's most famous culinary export. It typically consists of vinegared rice combined with various ingredients such as raw or cooked seafood, vegetables, and sometimes tropical fruits. Sushi is often served with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.


Sashimi

Sashimi refers to thinly sliced raw fish or seafood served without rice. It emphasizes the quality and freshness of the ingredients, often accompanied by soy sauce and wasabi.


Tempura

Tempura consists of lightly battered and deep-fried seafood, vegetables, or even mushrooms. The batter is typically made with flour, water, and sometimes egg, resulting in a crispy and light texture.


Ramen

Ramen is a popular noodle soup dish consisting of Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a flavorful broth, often flavored with soy sauce, miso, or salt, and topped with various ingredients such as sliced pork, green onions, nori (seaweed), and boiled eggs.


Katsudon

Katsudon is a popular Japanese dish that combines a deep-fried pork cutlet (tonkatsu) with eggs, onions, and a sweet and savory sauce, all served over a bowl of steamed rice.
